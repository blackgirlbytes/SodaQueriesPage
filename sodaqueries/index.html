Our Philopshy

Blacks make up 5% and latinos make up only 6.2% of the TOTAL “engineering workforce”. That means software engineers make up an EVEN SMALLER percentage. In 2018, there were only three black CEO's in the Fortune 500. This is saddening to us because we strongly believe diverse teams, both in skill and thought, create better products. Our own diverse backgrounds, in education, skill, interest, gender, and race drive us to help others succeed. We are graduates of the Resilient Coders bootcamp, which means we don't have traditional educational paths. We like anime, makeup, basketball, and natural hair. Most importantly, we like using our software development skills to get minority names out there.

Our Technique

The Individuals Behind the Cause

Cofounders and creatives, Rizel Bobb-Semple and Derek Scarlett learned full stack web development without attaining Computer Science degrees. In 2018, they graduated from the Resilient Coders bootcamp. Today, they are working as full time software developers in the corporate world. Currently, Rizel(on the left) sits on the alumni board for Hack Diversity to help increase the diversity in the Boston tech community. She also writes for Skillist and is working with GCode House to make technology education more accessible for women of color. Inherently a musician, Derek received his bachelor's in Music and Theater Arts with a specialization in Sound Recording Technology. Through music, he transforms abstract symbols into something tangible, which helped him to quick learn and love coding. Because of his passion for giving back, he spends his free time teaching kids to play the drums.
